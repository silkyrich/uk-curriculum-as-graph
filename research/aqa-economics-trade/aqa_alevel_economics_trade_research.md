## Content Requirements: Section 3.2.6 The International Economy

### 3.2.6.1 Globalisation
- Causes of globalisation
- Main characteristics
- Consequences for less-developed and more-developed countries
- Role of multinational corporations

### 3.2.6.2 Trade ← primary focus
- Model of comparative advantage
- Distinction between comparative and absolute advantage
- Specialisation and trade increasing total output
- Other benefits: economies of scale, increased competition
- Costs of international trade
- Changes in the pattern of UK trade with the rest of the world
- Nature of protectionist policies: tariffs, quotas, export subsidies
- Causes and consequences of protectionism
- Main features of a customs union
- Main characteristics of the Single European Market (SEM)
- Role of the World Trade Organisation (WTO)

**Spec additional information:**
- Students must be able to use a simple numerical example to illustrate comparative advantage
- Students must be able to draw and explain a tariff diagram

### 3.2.6.3 Balance of Payments
- Current, capital, financial accounts
- Current account components: trade in goods, services, primary income, secondary income
- Meaning of deficit/surplus
- Factors influencing current account: productivity, inflation, exchange rate
- Consequences of investment flows
- Policies to correct a BoP deficit or surplus
- Expenditure-switching and expenditure-reducing policies
- Effects of corrective policies on other macroeconomic objectives
- Significance of imbalances for individual economies and globally

**Spec additional information:**
- Detailed knowledge of current account required; only general appreciation of other accounts
- Must distinguish FDI from portfolio investment

### 3.2.6.4 Exchange Rate Systems
- Determination of exchange rates in freely floating systems
- Government intervention mechanisms
- Advantages/disadvantages of fixed vs floating systems
- Advantages/disadvantages of joining a currency union (e.g. eurozone)

### 3.2.6.5 Economic Growth and Development
- Growth vs development distinction
- Characteristics of less-developed economies
- Development indicators including HDI
- Factors affecting growth: investment, education, training
- Barriers to growth: corruption, institutions, infrastructure, human capital, property rights
- Policies to promote growth and development
- Role of aid and trade

## Assessment Structure

Trade is primarily examined in **Paper 2: National and International Economy (7136/2)**. Also appears synoptically in Paper 3.

| Paper | Duration | Marks | % of A-Level | Format |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Paper 1 (Markets & Market Failure) | 2 hours | 80 | 33.3% | Data response + essay |
| Paper 2 (National & International Economy) | 2 hours | 80 | 33.3% | Section A: data response (choice of 1 from 2, 40 marks); Section B: essay (choice of 1 from 3, 40 marks) |
| Paper 3 (Synoptic) | 2 hours | 80 | 33.3% | Section A: 30 MCQ; Section B: case study (50 marks) |

### Assessment Objectives (Paper 2 weightings)

| AO | Description | Paper 2 weighting |
|---|---|---|
| AO1 | Knowledge of terms, concepts, theories, models | 5–8% |
| AO2 | Apply knowledge to contexts | 7–10% |
| AO3 | Analyse economic problems | 9–11% |
| AO4 | Evaluate economic arguments, make informed judgements | 7–10% |

### Mark Allocations (from past papers/mark schemes)
- 2-mark: calculation questions
- 4-mark: "explain using data" (data response)
- 9-mark: "explain with a diagram" (levels-of-response, 3 levels — **no AO4 evaluation marks**)
- 15-mark: "explain/analyse" essay (3 levels — **no AO4 evaluation marks**)
- 25-mark: "evaluate/assess/consider whether" essay (5 levels — evaluation marks available)

**Key implication**: Evaluation is only rewarded in 25-mark questions. Students who evaluate in 9 or 15-mark answers gain no extra credit and lose time.

## Examiner Report: Common Student Errors and Misconceptions

Compiled from Nov 2020, Jun 2022, Jun 2023 reports and Jun 2022 annotated answers.

### Tariff Diagrams
- Axes not labelled correctly (recurring across all series)
- Students describe the diagram rather than explain the process — loses marks
- Confusing tariff introduction with tariff reduction
- Incorrect definition confusing imports and exports at the start of answers

### Protectionism
- Students focus only on tariffs/quotas; miss exchange rate depreciation and supply-side policies as expenditure-switching
- Confusing expenditure-switching with expenditure-reducing (recurring)
- Evaluating globalisation when asked specifically about trade

### Comparative Advantage
- Students "drift into comparative advantage" when answering FDI questions — conceptual boundary confusion
- Weaker responses focus on globalisation broadly rather than trade specifically

### Value vs Volume of Exports
- Students confuse "value of exports" with "price" — significant misconception
- Value = price × quantity (volume) must be explicit

### Currency Unions vs Trading Blocs
- Students answer about free trade areas/trading blocs when asked about currency unions — fundamental category confusion
- Missing the key constraint: joining a currency union removes monetary policy autonomy
- Many responses left unfinished — either time management or insufficient depth of knowledge

### Budget Deficit vs Current Account Deficit
- Persistent confusion between the government budget deficit and the current account deficit (recurring across all series)
- Must be explicitly taught as distinct concepts with different causes and policy responses

### Exchange Rates
- Weaker students don't understand monetary autonomy in fixed vs floating debate
- Not understanding that lower interest rates increase imports (exchange rate → competitiveness → imports chain)
- Writing "price" instead of "price level" on macroeconomic diagrams

### Use of Data in Data Response
- Quoting data without using it analytically — marks require data to develop arguments, not just cite
- Choosing unhelpful data points (obscure years, non-representative examples)
- Missing units on calculation answers ($ signs, "billion", decimal places)

### Essay Structure (25-mark)
- Theoretical analysis without application to the UK economy — top bands require contextual grounding
- Evaluation only in a final paragraph — top band requires "evaluation throughout and in a conclusion"
- Current affairs knowledge explicitly rewarded at higher bands

## Gap Analysis: What the Current Graph Can/Cannot Contribute

### What the graph CAN provide
- Pedagogical scaffolding: DifficultyLevel tiers (entry/developing/expected/greater_depth mapped to A-level = emerging/developing/secure/mastery)
- Interaction types: inference tasks, source analysis, categorisation — all relevant to A-level method
- ThinkingLens: Cause & Effect, Systems & Models, Evidence & Argument — direct matches to AO3/AO4
- VehicleTemplate patterns: case study, data response, essay — direct matches to Paper 2/3 formats
- SEND/AccessRequirement layer: language load, working memory — relevant for complex multi-concept questions
- Misconception modelling: the examiner report errors map well to the Misconception node type

### What the graph CANNOT currently provide
- The AQA spec content itself (comparative advantage, tariff diagrams, BoP structure) — not in the graph
- Exam question patterns and mark scheme language — not modelled
- Past paper question bank — not in the graph
- AO-level tagging of content — the graph has DifficultyLevel but not AO1/AO2/AO3/AO4 weighting
- Synoptic link mapping between Paper 1 (micro) and Paper 2 (macro) — the graph doesn't model this cross-paper dependency
- The "current affairs" component — top-band essays require real-world examples (post-Brexit trade patterns, etc.)

### Key insight for pipeline design
The graph's value at A-level is **pedagogical, not content**. The spec content must come from the AQA specification directly. The graph provides:
- How to scaffold the content (DifficultyLevel, interaction types)
- What cognitive moves to require (ThinkingLens → AO3/AO4)
- What barriers students will face (examiner report errors → Misconception/AccessRequirement nodes)
- What delivery format to use (VehicleTemplate → data response, essay, case study)

A-level pipeline proposal: **spec content + examiner misconceptions → graph pedagogical layer → materials**
